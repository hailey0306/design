Overview

PROBLEM STATEMENT

"Pics or it didn't happen!" These days, it seems like some people chronicle every living moment by regularly taking pictures.

However, It might be numerous similar looking photos or blurred ones in the albums, or people just have too many pictures in their phone so that they cannot find the photo that they need. Moreover, those photos occupied most of their smartphones'space.

THE GOAL

Running out of room on the phone-with no memory left for photos or videos, new apps, or data for the existing apps-can be a disaster. To avoid this scenario, design a mobile app to help users free up some space by clean up their phone album.

DURATION

5 Weeks

MY ROLE

UX Designer

Initial Idea

What's in my mind at the very beginning?

graph TD A[I have too many photos in my phone album.] --> B[My iCloud always asks me to buy new storage.] B -->C[Can someone help me to clean them?]

Inspired by google image search engine (you can simply drag an image to the search bar and search for similar images), my primary idea is to design an app that can automatically find out all the similar photos on our smartphone that allow us to choose what we want to keep then delete rest of them.

FIELD RESEARCH

Lost phone, lost memory.

77 percent of Americans now own smartphones, and many rely on them for memory support. People capture lots of important moments with their phones, so they need to make sure they're keeping those moments backed up.

The biggest problem for me is the storage of iCloud is too small, so I have to cancel the auto sync-service and connect the phone with PC every time.

The free space of iCloud online is only 5GB, which is limitable for many iOS users who plan to store their data from iOS device to iCloud, especially for the people who have more than one iOS device. If they don't want to pay $0.99-$19.99 for extra space every month, they need to free up their iCloud storage.

Target Audiences

"Tech-savvy" "

who loves to use smartphone to take photos.

There are two main types of users:

"Memorizer"

They are emotional, sensitive who are not entirely convinced by the technology. They are afraid of delete photos that record their important memories and they won't be able to get it back anymore.

"Decisiver"

They are decisive who are afraid of trouble, and they just want to delete some useless photos fast to free up their mobilephone's space to improve the performance of their mobilephones.

Thoughts

Although Streamline can help people clean up their phone album, the primary function relies on the technology that automatically finds similar photos. Also, clean up photos is like clean up our rooms. We all know that we should keep it organized, but we are always too lazy to do that. I think it's important to add some fun to Streamline. The next step for Streamline is to add another function that works the same way as Tinder, swipe left to delete the photo and swipe right to keep it. So users can clean up their photos as playing a small game when they are bored, waiting for the bus or eating alone.
