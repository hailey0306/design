Overview

Home-Away-From-Home Experience

Hotelzzz’s missions are to:
1. Delight guests with a great experience;
2. Emerge as a leader in the hospitality space;
3. Utilize technology to bring the industry into the digital world.

My Task:
Design a digital solution for Concierge/booking local tours

My Role:
Sole Designer

Duration:
3 Days

Concierge: Assist patrons at hotel, apartment, or office building with personal services. May take messages, arrange or give advice on transportation, business services or entertainment, or monitor guest requests for housekeeping and maintenance.

Design Preview

Explore Things to Do from Home Screen

Even though I am designing a product for users to book local tours, I still believe it's important to have a dashboard allow users to control and enjoy general amenities in the hotel.

View Upcoming Tour from Home Screen

After users booked the tour or event, they will be able to see their upcoming tours/events in the home screen.

Browse Hotel's Events in the Schedule/Calendar

If the user has already set their preferences in the account page, Hotelzzz will give recommendations about what to do in hotel base on the user's preferences.

Research

To narrow down the design scope, I divided hotel guests into three user types:

Business Travelers
75% of business travelers extend their stay for leisure. (Hotel Business)
Vacationers and Holidaymakers
Nearly 74% of domestic trips are for leisure purposes. (U.S. Travel)
Family Travelers
Gen Alpha is showing more signs of influencing family travel decisions and planning. (Short Term Rentalz)

 

 

Target Audience

Based on my researches, I decided to design for business travelers, because they are more likely to use the product.

They are “Digital Savvy”
98% of business travelers consider themselves to be advanced or intermediate users of digital tools. (Buying Business Travel)
They Book Tour with Hotels
59% of them booked the leisure part of the trip directly with the accommodation provider. (Hotel Business)

 

User Journey

I started by mapping out the current business traveler’s journey. I also listed all the touchpoints that might be related to concierge services and grouped all the information.

 

Problem Space

By interviewing a couple of users who have experiences booking tour with hotel during their business trip, I summarized user’s frustrations and goals.

Frustrations:
1.   The concierge won’t be able to fill guests’ needs if they are not physically in the hotel;
2.   Brochure don’t have enough detailed information: Specific time, driver, guide information;
3.   Undiscovered concierge offer: Restaurant reservation, special classes.
Goals:
1.   Make quick decision and book tour fast;
2.   Have a intuitive way to track their tours.

I also considered the goal of the concierge in my mind such as to give the best suggestions according to the guests' needs and make them happy, and their frustrations such as "Guests are getting the same questions asked dozens of times a day by dozens of hotel guests."

Solution

Problem Statement

Business travelers are drawn to extend their stay for leisure. They want to make fast decision to enjoy local tours or special events so they can make the most of their limited time. However, it's hard to book a tour or ask the concierge if they are not physically in the hotel. There needs a way to help guests receive home-away-from-home services on the go.

Wireframes

Scenario 1 - Book a tour
Serena is a product manager who is traveling for a design sprint in Puerto Rico. She arrived at the hotel and started to think about what she can do for leisure during her free time.

Scenario 2 - Check the tour detail

It is 4PM right now, Serena’s tour will be starting at 5:30PM, and she wants to know more details about the tour, such as the name of tour guide, which car she is going to take.

It is 4:55 PM right now, Serena's tour is coming, but she is shopping at San Juan Old Town right now. Hotelzzz concierge sends her a reminder - “it's time to go back to the hotel.”

Scenario 3 - Recommendations

Based on Serena's schedule and preferences, Hotelzzz will give recommendations for her.

 

Iterations

Decision - 1
Compare to read multiple messages to get all tips, it’s better to let users check all the details and tips by themselves.

Decision - 2
Same layout might be hard for user to distinguish different sections.

Decision - 3
The upcoming schedule should be in the home page because business travelers care more about their schedule.
