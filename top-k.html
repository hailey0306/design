Overview

Client & Project

SayHomee is a software company based in the San Francisco Bay Area that works with experienced contractors/handyman, providing services including bathroom remodeling, kitchen remodeling, ADU's and more.

When SayHomee came to me, they brought me the key information they've collected from the project -- Top Kitchen & Bath Website Redesign

Users: 40 ~ 60 years old people and part of younger engineers

Design style: Modern, simple and light luxury

Corporation Spirit: Reliable, High quality, Good service

My Role: Interaction Designer

Analysis

Understanding

The first step that I took was to familiarise myself with the home remodeling business. From the document they shared with me and visiting their website, I learned that their business goal is to attract more local homeowners. And this company specializes in kitchen, bathroom, and ADU(Accessory Dwelling Units).

Original Design


Key Problems

  • The main call to action is not clear on the home page;
  • Information architecture needs to reorganize;
  • The user flow is not straightforward: too many clicks needed to get information.
First Delivery

In the original design, there is a couple of duplicated content on the top navigation and required users to hover and click to view more information. From a UX perspective, today's users prefer to scroll rather than click. Also, there was too much information above the page fold without any branding elements.

To increase the bar for the home remodeling site, I decided to set a tone at the very beginning and reserve the top of the page for high-priority content: 30 years of home remodeling experience.

Then, I connected "Our Services," "Recent Projects" "How it Works," "Testimonials," and a "Contact form" to streamline the main workflow.

New Landing Page Design


Client Feedbacks

"It's too plain."

The client doesn't like my design at the very beginning, even though I explained my rationale. The only feedback she gave to me is "it's too plain." By showed different competitor's site to the client, I figured out that she prefers full-width layout and wants to add some interactions to the site since most of her customers are work in tech.

Conclusion

Thrive as a middle person

The big challenge for this project is that I am not directly designing for my client but their client. So, for this project, my role is a middle person. It's crucial to educate the end client on some basic UX principles and trends but also meet their expectations.

Steve Jobs once said, "People don't know what they want until you show it to them". There is a gap between most of the clients and designers. The key insights to thrive in this project are:

  • 1. Show them different options to figure it out what the end client wants exactly;

  • 2. Try your best to show a realistic prototype, not limited to one tool.

  • 3. Balance the needs between client and project if you are the middle person.
