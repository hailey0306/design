Profile Page

Ever since Do Good Points launched last December, we’ve been working on improving our products. Finally, all of the hard work is starting to bear fruit. However, our "profile page" is still in its MVP version, and we now believe that it is necessary to create a new profile page to keep our users engaged.

Overview

Where to find all my information?

Do Good Points is a first-of-its-kind rewards community. When members complete “do good” actions, we reward them with credits called Good Points. Good Points can then be redeemed for gift cards from Amazon, Starbucks, and Target. In addition, they can also be used to donate cash to other charities in need.

After the last implement, our website's bounce rate decreased by 13% and the completes rate increased by 5%. However, we still have a long way to go, there are a lot of problems need to be solved, such as discoverability - some of our users asked where are their points, and the user retention - we didn't have much return users.

Needs

User - "I want to find my personal information quickly and easily."

Stakeholder - "We want to collect more user data."

Research

Why a new profile page?

CONTEXTUAL INQUIRY

A user profile is a collection of settings and information associated with a user. Every user should have their own profile page which may be reached from the top right menu (My Account - My profile page). This page contains links to further pages allowing the user to edit their profile information and preferences, manage their behavior in the site.

Who could benefit from the user profile page?

Website owners: since these records serve as the sources of valuable customer data, website owners can better target marketing activities and increase profits.

Users: they will be able to receive personalized product/service recommendations, check their activities history and manage their preferences.

In this case, I listed two types of user:

  • Who signed up but never donate; ( 0 Points )
  • Who signed up and donated at least once. ( 250+ Points )

What do users expect to see on the profile page?

Mental models are the assumptions people carry in their minds before interacting with a website or application based on past experience.

Information is easier to discover when it is in a place that matches the user's mental model of where it should be.

--Nielsen Norman Group

PROBLEMS

  • There is a gap between what our users expect and what we used to have.

    • Research suggests that first impressions are critical in determining whether or not to trust someone, and that when a trust breach occurs, it can be difficult to restore.

  • Users cannot check their recent activities including donation history, redemption history, and points status.

    • Meantime, we should focus on competence: letting users know that they’re intelligent, that they’re capable, that they have the ability to act on whatever their intentions are. Hence, within the account pages, users are extremely “task-oriented,” with a single goal in mind. For example, “changing my password,” “looking up my latest donation,” “checking my points status,” etc.

GOALS

  • Create a central hub for users to manage their personalized data and preferences;
  • Collect user data for effective marketing;
  • Build user loyalty and encourage them to return.
Solution

PRODUCT SCALE

We are a small start-up company, so we don't have much of a budget or the time to dedicate towards a large-scale design. I listed what I wanted to be covered in this improvement and discussed this with our development and marketing teams to set the stage for finalizing the content that we need at this current stage.

  • Greetings : Name + Email

    • I believe that greetings (customized messages) are one of the most effective ways of engaging and converting website visitors. This email is to notify our users that they have the power to choose which email address they use with us.

  • Achievements : Current Points Balance + Points In Process

    • By using a "Points Bar" to show the users' current points balance and points in process gives them a visual look into the number of points that they can redeem.


  • Profiler : General Information

    • Another designer from my team, Hua, designed a series of profiler questions to collect more user-data for future design and marketing use.


  • Recent Activites : Donation History + Redumption History



  • Account Setting : Change Password

TESTING

The big challenge for an early stage company is that we had no user data at all when we first began, let alone the budget for formal user testing. Most of the testing that I conducted were from random people that I invited from church, Starbucks, and my friends. Here are the key findings:

Iteration 1

Woohoo!

Achievements Bar

Instead of the confusing points bar, I designed an achievement bar which includes "current points balance", "total donations made", and "lifetime points earned". I also removed the profile image and changed it to "Do Good'er". We are not a non-profit company, but we partner with those charities. We believe we are doing good, and we want our users to feel the same way! In the meantime, we want them to keep donating and keep doing good.

General Information

I redesigned the visual design of the profiler questions by moving the process bar to the button because I saw a survey that shows that progress bars shown at the bottom of the page improved the completion rate. Also, I redesigned the general information form to a shorter way.

Activities History

To help users check the donation and redemption history clearer and more comfortable, I added a split light grey background to the form.

Iteration 2

After creating the new design, I did a second round testing. Nobody can design the perfect user interface, there is no guarantee that the new design does in fact fix the problems. The second test might discover whether the fixes worked or whether they didn't. Also, in introducing a new design, there is always the risk of introducing a new usability problem, even if the old one did get fixed.

Original Design Problem

The way I designed for users to edit their profile information is confusing. The general information came from the survey questions that are either single choices or multiple choices, so the user shouldn't be able to edit by typing the answer directly.

Conclusion

Impact

Helps more than 5K Do Good Points users.

The drop rate on the profile page decreased by 23.8%, and the completes rate of the survey question is 69.23%.

Think About the Scalability

User experience is the primary driver for competition today. However, when there are limitations on budget and time, it's hard to decide which features should be implemented first.

We want to strive to achieve the highest amount of growth in the shortest period of time. In order to do that, we need to maximize our resources and fully communicate with different team members to ensure that we have the same goal. The most important goal for us is to design a product with scalability for further development.
