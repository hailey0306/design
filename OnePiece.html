OnePiece Work

OnePiece is an innovative co-working space and cross-border incubator founded in the heart of Silicon Valley. This is a Design Jam project that my team designed for OnePiece Work to help them build a better brand image and attract more startup companies. We won the 1st place in the design contest.

Overview

Startup Incubator

After our research about the competitors like WeWork and Bespoke, we found out that OnePiece Work not only provides their members with high-quality office services, but also offer strategic consulting, access to funding, and global expansion services. And we saw a significant potential in their mentorship program that can help them build an entrepreneur leader brand image and attract more start-up company to use their product as well.

I was in a team of six and responsible for the product side. ( We only have about 7 hours, including lunch time, to finish our design, so we separated our team to product and visual side after ideation.)

During the ideation, before we decide our final direction, I was responsible for make sure our team does not lose focus. ( Idea sessions can easily dissolve into wandering and woolgathering.) After that, I was contributed to do the user flow, sketching, low-fi wireframing and hi-fi interface design.

To comply with my non-disclosure agreement, I have omitted and obfuscated confidential information in this case study. All information in this case study is my own and does not necessarily reflect the views of OnePiece Work.

Research

CONTEXTUAL INQUIRY

The first step we took was to familiarise ourselves with OnePiece Work and the way the services they offer. From the document they shared with us and visiting their website, we learned their business goal is to attract more small business and startup companies. They accelerate the growth of small business through their extensive mentor and partner networks.

PERSONA

We decided to focus on the early-stage startup companies based on our research and my experiences at startup companies. And We summarized their goals:

  • Finding the Right People
  • Sourcing Founding
  • Looking for Experts
  • Getting a Nice Work Space

What OnePiece Work Can Offer & HMWs

  • How might we better connect startup companies with OnePiece Work?
  • How might we attract startup companies to join the mentorship program?
  • How might we promote OnePiece Work's brand image?
  • How might we promote OnePiece Work's co-working space?

We also reviewed what other co-working space companies are offering available on the market. We realized that OnePiece Work is the only one who is offering mentorship programs, so we want to concentrate on redesign the mentorship part.

Our Solution

Mentorship program is attractive but there is no good entry point for startup companies. As a result, our main goal is to help OnePiece Work to improve the resource discovery, which including:

  • Tech Focused Mentorship
  • Office Space
  • Talent
  • Event

Conclusion

Think About the Business

This was my first hackathon ever, and I wanted to see how I would handle an unfamiliar situation with new teammates in a problem space I knew very little about.

During a hackathon, with only a couple hours to build a product, we need to do user research and market research. While someone is conducting interviews, someone should also be looking at the latest market trends to see if what weâ€™re about to make is going to make any actual money.

At times it was stressful working with a team I barely knew, but it was much better than what I thought. Our team members are creative, critical and openminded. We cared about everyone's ideas and concern, and we seek common ground. Our team didn't focus too much on the actual design of our product but pay more attention to the viability and business value.That's was one of the best teamwork that I've experienced.

This hackathon taught me to consider the needs and opportunities of the business, always, because (from a business perspective) what is the point of building something if no one is going to use it.
