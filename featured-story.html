Featured Story

BACK TO TOP

Overview

Do Good Points is a platform that donors can donate to the charity and earn cash value points to donate again. The goal is to help individuals discover the cause that they care about and to help the charity raise more money. To achieve that, we decided to focus especifically on the "discovery experience" -- adding some charity-related content that users can engage with to create a Good Community.

Currently, we have 10k+ users, and there are 5k+ donations that have been made on our site.

Problem: Less than 1% return users.

Design Goal: Increase the user retention.

My Role: As a sole designer in the company, I worked closely with the CEO and the developers to validate ideas quickly and build a foundation for the new product.

Discover

Previous Research

Users want more information.

I found a research shows that our target audience researches the charity impact before donating. According to givingtuesday.org, 79% of millennials researched a charity before donating.

 
 

Do we have the information on our site that they are rearching for? I conducted a focus group to hear their voices.


  • "There is no much information on this site for me to explore."

  • "I am not sure the site is trustful or not yet."

  • "I already donated to the charity that I trusted, haven't seen enough information make me trust other charities."

Early Iterations

Based on our research results and the information we collected from our competitors like GoFundMe and Kickstarter, we did multiple testing and iterations: we added more related images; we listed some impacts that the organization has made and is planning to do.



The result shows that it only increased the first time donation rate but didn't bring many users back. What was missing? What does retention mean in our site? If we want to keep users wanting to do more when using our platform, should we focus on user engagement? What can they engage with right now besides donating?


New Research

What are they looking for?

Clue 1

Then I found something interesting: those one-time donors actually saved 3-4 charities in their "favorites", but only donated to one of them. Why? With these questions in mind, I decided to delve even more profound to our users by having another round of user research.


 
 

Clue 2

There are not just "donors" and "non-donors".

Before, I thought there were only two groups - donors and non-donors. I can't say it was wrong. However, when I went deeper, I realized that people have different ways and levels to support the causes they care about rather than donate. Here is my survey result about people's behavior about donating in the last 12 months. 23% of people chose "I support this cause but have not donated." What does that mean?



Clue 3

In order to understand the motivation of donors and the reasons why people don't donate, I conducted individual interviews to identify the problems better.


  • "I donated when I felt lucky, but I think I need to save money for myself and for my family."

  • "I prefer to donate to the immediate causes such as an earthquake or wildfire just happened."

  • "I am too busy with my work and social life so I joined a monthly donation plan. I don’t have time and money to find another charity to donate right now."

  • "I don't know what else I can do on the site after donating."

  • "I just don't donate that often."

By talking to real users individually, I realized that some of our users are not donating that often because they don't have enough money and they might be interested in something else to do on our site beside donating.


Define

Personas

I summarized three personas as a reference to better define the problem and picked our primary target audience - young professionals to dedign for.



Insights

1. Donating might not be a daily behavior for them, but Do Good could be.

People believe that donation is not the only way to give back. Some of them already Do Good in their daily life, such as keep classificating waste and offering homeless some food.

2. They tent to donate to the charity that they care about the most.

Most of our users supported at least two causes, and they donated to the one that motivated them the most. Their motivations might be related to their own experience or the news on TV and social media they have seen.

Develop

Our Solution

Words are power

Rather than creating something new for users to adapt, I believe that it's necessary to focus on what they are doing every day: they check their email around 15 times; they prefer to read the news on digital; they spend around 2 hours on social media; they like to interact meaningfully and contribute work as well...

 
 

By analyzing our users' daily behaviors, we decided to start with "featured articles/videos". The goal is to knowledge our user causes first, and then influence them to donate.



Partnering with non-profits and charities, we post featured articles/videos - trying to be as relevant as possible - for users to explore everyday. I want to help them find the cause that they care about. In the meantime, by engaging with the story or other users, I want our users to feel that they are in a Good Community.



Challenges & Ideas

To incentivize our users to engage with the featured story, We also reward them when they finish reading by giving them points. But the problem is how to measure if they finished the reading?

Option 1:   When they scroll down to the end of the article;
Option 2:   After 2 mins they have stayed on the page;
Option 3:   When a checkbox at the end of the article is checked.

Decision:   Ask a question based on the featured content type.



By submitting the answer, we admit they have finished reading and collected the data (their attitude about this content). Based on what the user liked, we can feed them the content that they are most likely to engage with later.

Fill The Gaps

Connecting the dots

We want to create for our users the end to end experience: learn the cause, join the discussion, share the story to friends, learn the charity and donate.



Each time users engage with or support causes they care about, they earn Good Points that can be donated to charities of their choice. That's how we see the full Do Good cycle:



Challenges & Ideas:

How to avoid some users trying to earn plenty of points at onetime?

  • Delete the article/story in 7 days;
  • Set a limitation for earning by reading every day.

How to avoid some users posting useless, even garbage comments?

  • Monitoring all the comments and filter out the garbage comments;
  • Set a limitation for earning by commenting every day.

How to control their sharing behavior?

  • Add one more step before they can share;
  • Set a limitation for earning by sharing ever yday.

How to notice them that they have earned Good Points?

  • Micro-animations;
  • Notifications.

Solutions:

It was more difficult and more complicated than I thought to set a limitation for all the behaviors. I worked with our back-end developers and accountant to finalize the rules.



We used google sheets to list all the functions, caps, and points earnings as well as how we collect and show the data in the activity bell and donation history page.

Reflection

Learn by doing

It's the first time that I own the whole end-to-end design process with such a big project. As a junior designer, I know there might be some shortcomings in this project, especially within a super early stage start-up team. But that's how I learned to use limited resources to build a new product with all the business constraints. I closely worked with our CEO to understand the business model and goals. By joining the sales calls, I cognized what our partners' needs. To better collaborate with the engineers, sometimes I have to think about the user's scenarios from a developer's perspective by learning from them. It's essential to learn from the senior team members, but I would like to hold my opinions as I'm speaking for our users.
