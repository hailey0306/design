Overview

The Challenges

As a sole designer, I refreshed the branding for a younger generation and designed interfaces that thousands of users use to make online donations.

When I joined Do Good Points, the company just launched the MVP. It was built rapidly to test the product in the market. My responsibilities were to:

  1. refresh the branding to fit our target audience;
  2. correct existing UX issues;
  3. continue to build out new features.

Background

Asking for donations — it’s intimidating. We get it.

Do Good Points is the first of its kind rewards community. We are not a non-profit who ask for money directly. We want people to come to our site to explore the organizations that they like then make the decision. But why should they come to our site?

1. Discover.

There are more than 1.5 million non-profit organizations registered in the U.S. We want people to come to our site to explore charities and get general information so that they can choose which charity they wish to donate to.

2. Reward.

For every donation that people make from our site, they can earn points for rewards.

3. Record.

We know this question will come during tax season: “Did you make any charitable contributions?” Of course. Did you document that donation? Sigh. Maybe not well enough. Even though we do not make documentation of your donations, we can keep records of the different organizations you donated to so you can track the amounts you paid out to the charities.

Challenge 1

What should our brand look like?

Target Audience

According to the Chronicle of Philanthropy, baby boomers account for 41 percent of charitable giving. Facing with an aging and shrinking donor base, some nonprofits are taking steps to explore new fundraising approaches. Our goal is to bring younger donors -- millennials, to those charities.

Original Branding

As a bridge to connect a younger generation with charities, it's important to represent us as a hip brand. However, the original branding of Do Good Points is not fit for millennials based on the feedback we gathered from our users. We need to have fresher and emerging branding.

New Branding

The stakeholders and I chose 3 out of 40 words to represend our branding.

By starting with the mood board, I picked four colors to be our brand color. I recolored the logo instead of redesign from scratch, for not confusing our exisiting users.

Why is our conversion rate so low?

As we just launched our MVP, we didn't have personas, and we want to wait until we see some typical patterns exit. What we have is a general target audience -- millennials, and at that stage, we were trying to find the pain points from new users.

USER JOURNEY MAP

To help the team understand and address customer needs, I led a workshop to create a journey map for our current product.

We found out that certain places might cause a low conversion rate during the overall experience. Based on the user flow, through traffic, average page time view and drop-offs data, I decided to work on some small changes on our discover page first.

Challenge 3

Where to find all my information?

After the last implementation, our website's bounce rate decreased by 13% and the completes rate increased by 5%. However, we still have a long way to go, there are a lot of problems need to be solved, such as discoverability - some of our users asked where their points are, and the user retention - we didn't have much return users.

Needs

User - "I want to find my account related information quickly and easily."

Stakeholder - "We want to collect more user data."

MY APPROACH

"How about createing a new profile page?"

OUR (ORIGINAL) PROFILE PAGE

PROBLEMS

  • There is a gap between what our users expect and what we used to have.

    • Research suggests that first impressions are critical in determining whether or not to trust someone, and that when a trust breach occurs, it can be difficult to restore.

  • Users cannot check their recent activities including donation history, redemption history, and points status.

    • Meantime, we should focus on competence: letting users know that they’re intelligent, that they’re capable, that they have the ability to act on whatever their intentions are. Hence, within the account pages, users are extremely “task-oriented,” with a single goal in mind. For example, “changing my password,” “looking up my latest donation,” “checking my points status,” etc.

GOALS

  • Create a central hub for users to manage their personalized data and preferences;
  • Collect user data for effective marketing;
  • Build user loyalty and encourage them to return.

Solution

  • Greetings : Name + Email
  • Achievements : Current Points Balance + Points In Process
  • Profiler : General Information
  • Recent Activites : Donation History + Redumption History
  • Account Setting : Change Password
