Overview

PROBLEM STATEMENT

The battery of our electrical devices is never enough. There are many ways people can charge their electrical devices and the most common solution is to carry a charger or a powerbank (portable charger), but you've got to remember to charge the powerbank, and power plugs are often few and far between.

THE GOAL

In order to improve the charging experience for city people, we want people can easy to rent a powerbank and then return it when they finished charging.

MY ROLE

UX Researcher, Mobile Designer and Powerbank Product Design. (Team: 4 members)

DURATION

5 Weeks

Initial Idea

BRANDSTORMING

As a group, we brainstormed together, by starting at "low battery", we presented to each other with the problem that needs to be solved without the feasibility issues. We listed all the ways that people can get charged and then analyzed different situations that people might face too. We also summarized the merits & drawbacks of all ideas.

To find more status to support our idea and eliminate ideas that are not feasible, we started our marketing research.

Research

DESKTOP RESEARCH

To better define the problem that we’re attempting to solve and articulating why those issues are essential, I did some desktop research. Here are the highlights:

Nearly 9 out of 10 people "felt panic" when their phone battery drops to 20 percent or lower;


32% of them will "drop everything" to head home and charge phones;

33% have delayed a date or meeting because of a low battery;

22% of them ordering something at a bar or restaurant just to use their power outlet;

FIELD RESEARCH

I also went to cafes, shopping centers, transportation stations, and airports to observe how people are charging their electrical devices and I also interviewed some of the people that I observed to explore our potential users and their wants and needs.

Public cafes like Starbucks are the main places that people outside of their office and home to charge their devices. However...

Our Users

Target Audience

Primary

The primary target audience is the tourism market. Both personal vacation and business traveling have a demand for using electronic devices such as laptop computer, digital camera, and portable game devices during their trip. It is essential to have a stable continuous power supply and Internet access especially when they rely on navigation application so much during the traveling.

 

Secondary

The secondary target audience will be the people who normally work outside of home or office but still spend a lot of time on their electrical devices, and the group who spend hours on their mobile phones but don’t like to charge their phones. Sometimes they forget to carry their phone charger or power bank all the time.

Thoughts

APM is not just a combination of different technologies, and it's more about improving the whole charging experience. To approach this case study, I tried to go deeper and explore human behavior then turned data into design decisions and articulate each.

Through this project, I realized that a successful product or service is not only for the client but should also work well within society. Inspired by sharing economy, I wanted to create a business module that can benefit users, companies and the environment which can lead to a triple win.
